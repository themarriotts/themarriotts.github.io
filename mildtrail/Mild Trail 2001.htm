Sweet Mild Of Mine 

Mild is an English ale that is only lightly hopped and therefore not normally bitter in flavour. Milds are often low in alcohol (though Sarah Hughes is an obvious exception to this rule) and often dark due to the use of treacly malts (although Banks’s is an obvious exception here.)

Why Drink Mild?

Well, let me see. It tastes great, it’s cheap, it’s low strength makes it the perfect session beer, and if you don’t drink it now you may not have the chance later. So there are plenty reasons to drink it, so why don’t people try it?

Image?

It’s seen as an old mans drink and little effort is made to promote it. When was the last time you saw a Mild ad on the TV? Don’t believe the hype. Give it a go.

Quality?

People think that the slops are often re-cycled through the Mild. In the modern pub this extremely difficult to do. If you are drinking a bad pint of Mild chances are the whole barrel is off. Mild’s low strength does not help in preserving it, hence Mild has to be sold in a pub where this beer is popular and there is a good turnover. Our Mild Trail pubs will give you ample opportunity to try Mild at it’s best.

A Dying Beer Style?

Mild is a dying beer style and is slowly disappearing. In many parts of the country it has long since gone. In the Midlands it still has a strong following and can be found in most areas however this will only continue to be the case if people keep drinking it. Hence we at Rugby CAMRA and CAMRA as a whole seek to promote this excellent beer style in any way we can.
